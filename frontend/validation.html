Each era bot vs. the historical record it was trained on. ← back to the board
Method. Each bot played 150 self-play games at its serving temperature. The same analyzer computed identical, move-sequence-based metrics on the bot games and on random samples of the historical era corpora (Lumbra's Gigabase, OTB games) — no ECO tags, no cherry-picking. Resignation is adjudicated from the model's own win-probability head, since humans resign lost positions and policy networks otherwise grind to mate. Draw agreements are adjudicated the same way: a bot agrees once the evaluation has sat in a dead-equal band for an era-specific stretch of moves — the Soviet school concedes readily, the Classical era is moderate, Romantics almost never.

Honest residuals. One systematic gap remains, and one has largely closed. First moves lean 1.e4 beyond the historical rate: the bots are fine-tuned from Maia-2, which was trained on modern online chess — one epoch of era data moves its opening prior a long way, but not all the way. Decisive games still run ~15–20 plies longer than historical ones: the bots resign, but less promptly than masters did. The draw gap, once the larger residual, is now modeled: bots offer and agree draws from the same win-probability signal, with era-specific willingness — and bot draw rates land within ~1.5 points of history in all three eras (including the Soviet-era "grandmaster draw" culture). Neither remaining gap affects the in-period style signal above.
